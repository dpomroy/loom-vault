# Notes: Thomas Laffont: The $4T AI IPO Wave, 2026's Unicorn Economy, and the 10X Paradox

## Summary

**Coatue's Thomas Laffont** presents a data-driven
state-of-the-union on the private tech ecosystem.

Core finding: the unicorn economy is **healthier**
than the ZIRP era — fewer companies, each raising
more, with real revenue and imminent IPOs.

The "Magnificent Eight" private cos (~$4T combined)
are outperforming the public Mag 7. SpaceX, Anthropic,
and OpenAI IPOs alone could return more liquidity than
the prior decade combined.

The 10x paradox: **scale accelerates advantage** —
centacorns (>$100B) have a 31% chance of a further
10x, vs 8% for unicorns. Power law is intensifying.

Key risk: if liquidity floods back into the ecosystem,
a price war between OpenAI and Anthropic isn't
unthinkable — echoing ride-sharing and food delivery.

## Key Arguments

1. **The unicorn economy has structurally improved**
since ZIRP — fewer, larger, more capital-efficient
companies with real revenue and near-term IPO paths.

2. **Power law is accelerating, not flattening** —
centacorns have a 31% shot at a further 10x,
making late concentration into proven winners
a rational (if uncomfortable) strategy.

3. **SpaceX's value-per-launch rising is rational**:
it reflects a business model transitioning from
one-time launches → recurring constellation
revenue → platform, each stage worth more than
the last.

4. **AI revenue is real and quantifiable**: ~$140B
today, ~$300B this year, doubling by 2027 — split
across consumer subscriptions, AI-enabled ads
(25% of Meta/Google inventory, headed to 100%),
and enterprise.

5. **The biggest unpredictable risk is a price war**:
once IPO liquidity recycles back into the ecosystem,
OpenAI or Anthropic could weaponise capital the way
Uber and DoorDash did — destroying margins across
the sector before sustainable unit economics emerge.
