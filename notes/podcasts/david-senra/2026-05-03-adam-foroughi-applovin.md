# Notes: Adam Foroughi, AppLovin

## Summary

AppLovin's Adam Foroughi built a $140B+ ad-tech company
by rejecting almost every conventional playbook:
no VCs, no board for six years, no CRO, no bloat.

**The core insight:** performance marketing should make
advertisers into arbitrage machines — spend $1,
get back more than $1, scale until the bank runs dry.

The inflection came in April 2023 when Axon 2
(deep learning, not just ML) shipped. Stock went
from ~$9 to a peak of $750. The $6B buyback
at the bottom — leveraged, contrarian, targeting
known sellers — turned into ~$60B of value.

Foroughi's edge isn't the tech. It's the operating
philosophy: hire people with chips on their
shoulders, keep the org ruthlessly lean, and
personally approve every new hire so headcount
can never drift upward on autopilot.

## Quotes

### "to lever up to buy your own shares when everyone's telling you your company is a piece of shit, that's really scary to do."

The quote lands because the fear was rational —
stock down 92%, yet he borrowed to buy more.
The contrarian move only works if you trust your
own fundamentals over the market's signal.

---

### "if no one's going to buy our shares, why don't we just start buying our own shares?"

Simple logic, hard to execute under pressure.
Most founders in a 92% drawdown go defensive.
Foroughi went offensive with leverage. The gap
between knowing this and doing it is enormous.

---

### "I didn't go to VCs until knew we were going to be an advertising business."

This is the anti-pitch-deck move. He bootstrapped
through the messy pivot phase, only raising when
there was real product-market fit. Means he never
had to defend bad early decisions to investors.

---

### "find people who have a chip on their shoulder, someone who has a reason to push hard."

This is his actual hiring filter — not credentials,
not pedigree. It explains the high school dropout
(Raf) who became a billionaire deal-maker. Hunger
is the variable most hiring processes screen out.

---

### "my family was uprooted out of Iran... my dad had one of the most successful real estate development companies in the country... and we got booted out of our country."

The origin of the chip. His father lost everything
to revolution, and Foroughi internalised that as
obligation, not just ambition. That's a different
quality of drive — harder to turn off.

---

### "I didn't even know what a state owned business was at the point in time"

The China deal nearly destroyed the company not
through malice but through ignorance of geopolitics.
A board would have flagged CFIUS immediately.
This is the clearest case he makes for why
six years without one was a mistake.

---

### "A players don't like to work with B, Cs, Ds, I mean, Ds, you're gonna just burn your A's out."

The compounding cost of a mediocre hire isn't
the mediocre hire — it's the A player who leaves
because of them. Most orgs never price this in.
Foroughi did, and fired 40% of the team while
growing revenue ~100% YoY.

---

### "we went from somewhere in the neighborhood of hundreds of new hires that we're doing a year, or at least attempting to do a year, to in the tens because people were so spooked about trying to convince me that they need the role, they wouldn't Come to me."

Forcing managers to justify headcount to the CEO
is blunt but effective. The friction is the feature.
If a manager won't fight for the hire, they probably
didn't need it. This is how you stop autopilot bloat.

---

### "one person who's really good can be as powerful as 10, 20, 50, 100 people."

He's not making a vague AI point here — he's using
it as a **reason not to hire ahead of demand**.
Most companies treat AI as a productivity multiplier
on existing headcount. He treats it as a reason
to keep headcount flat or shrinking.

---

### "your 1x engineer might be 2x more efficient, but your 10x engineer might be 100x more efficient."

The non-linearity is the key claim. AI doesn't
lift everyone equally — it amplifies judgment,
not just output. A-players know how to use it;
B-players generate more mediocre output faster.
This is worth sitting with.

---

### "if we get the local laundromat discovered by someone playing a game because we serve a really good ad to someone who needs their clothes wash... this business is going to be much, much bigger than it is today."

This is the long-term bull case stated plainly.
Gaming as a distribution layer for **all** SMB
advertising — not just e-commerce. Whether the
targeting gets precise enough is the open question,
but the surface area of the opportunity is real.

## Key Arguments

1. **The buyback was the trade of the decade.**
   At $3.8B market cap with $1B+ EBITDA, Foroughi
   leveraged up to buy back ~$6B of stock from
   known sellers (private equity), not the open
   market — generating ~$60B in value.

2. **No board for six years was both advantage
   and liability.** It let him reject a $600M
   acquisition offer without pressure. It also
   let him nearly destroy the company with a
   Chinese investment that triggered CFIUS review.

3. **The Axon 2 model (April 2023) was the real
   business inflection.** Deep learning replaced
   traditional ML and made the performance promise
   actually work at scale: plug in, spend money,
   get measurably more back.

4. **Lean org structure is a deliberate moat.**
   ~400 people generating ~$5B/year in cash.
   No CRO, no COO. Every hire requires CEO sign-off.
   The constraint forces quality over headcount.

5. **Gaming is just the first vertical.**
   The platform logic — billion adults, 35-second
   full-screen ads, performance-only model — applies
   to any advertiser category. E-commerce is already
   working. SMB and enterprise are the stated next move.
