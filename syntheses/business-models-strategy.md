# Monopoly Logic: Why Structure Determines Destiny

These sources converge on a single uncomfortable truth: durable competitive advantage is structural, not operational. Costco's 'scale economies shared with customers' describes a self-reinforcing loop where cost savings become loyalty become scale. Rolex's optimal price-quantity positioning shows that finding the right curve matters more than executing on the wrong one. Thiel states it most bluntly — every great company solves a unique problem and escapes competition entirely. Where they diverge is on time horizon: Rolex's foundation structure explicitly allows three bad years without consequence, insulating strategy from quarterly pressure. Most companies, as Thiel implies, never even ask whether they'll exist in a decade.
