# Monopoly Logic: Price, Scarcity, and Structural Moats

These sources converge on a single underlying truth: durable competitive advantage requires a deliberate structural choice, not incremental optimization. Costco's 'scale economies shared' model and its iron discipline against price increases are the same strategy as Ferrari's artificial scarcity — both companies resist the obvious short-term revenue grab to protect long-term positioning. Thiel frames this abstractly: monopolies solve unique problems; competitors are, by definition, copying. Where the sources diverge is on mechanism. Costco wins by radical price transmission to customers. Ferrari wins by withholding supply entirely. Both work, but they serve opposite customer psychologies — one competes on value, the other on exclusion.
