# Small Teams, High Standards, Brutal Clarity

These sources converge on a counterintuitive truth: organizational performance scales inversely with headcount when quality is uncompromising. Kelly Johnson's rule — restrict people 'in an almost vicious manner' — mirrors Foroughi's warning that A players burn out surrounded by B and C players, and Chesky's insight that great people are self-managing, making heavy recruitment a substitute for heavy management. Where they differ is in emphasis: Johnson focuses on structural smallness as a design principle for breakthrough work, Foroughi on talent density as a multiplier, and Catmull on the leader's role as room-dynamics manager rather than output producer. Together they suggest that org size is a leadership choice, not a scaling inevitability.
