# Scarcity, Control, and the Myth Machine

Across the NFL, Ferrari, and Formula 1, the same underlying logic appears: value is manufactured through deliberate control of supply, narrative, and perception. The NFL's Rozelle understood that the league's stature — not any individual team — was the product to protect. Ferrari literalizes this with a policy of always selling one car fewer than demand. Ecclestone took it furthest: he controlled Formula 1 for decades without ever owning it, because control of the story was the real asset. Ferrari's Luca di Montezemolo made the same conceptual shift explicit — from racing company to luxury brand. The insight these sources share is that the myth is the business, and the myth requires active, ruthless management.
