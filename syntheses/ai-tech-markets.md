# Compute Scarcity, Model Moats, and the Enterprise Race

Compute is the central constraint shaping the AI industry's trajectory. Anthropic's CFO frames it as existential: too much and you go bankrupt, too little and you fall behind the frontier. That scarcity underpins massive capital commitments — OpenAI targeting $120B+, GPU demand that would exhaust supply through 2026. Yet even as infrastructure spending accelerates, there's disagreement about where durable value accrues. The commodity-models narrative has been 'thoroughly disproven' by Anthropic's $9B-to-$30B run-rate leap in a single quarter, suggesting frontier intelligence retains pricing power. But Nikesh Arora cuts the other way: models will become a utility layer, and profit pools sit in applications. These views aren't fully contradictory — frontier model labs may sustain margins while the broader model layer commoditizes — but the question of who captures enterprise spend as AI diffuses through Fortune 500 workflows remains genuinely open.
