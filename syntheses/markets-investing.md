# Public Markets as the Ultimate Truth Test

These sources converge on a shared conviction: markets, over time, are ruthless arbiters of reality. The OpenAI CFO frames public markets as a weighing machine that ultimately ignores narrative momentum — first-mover buzz fades, fundamentals remain. Laffont echoes this, calling public markets 'the great antiseptic' that strips away private-market flattery. Bill Maris adds a structural dimension: the math of large venture funds is quietly broken, with returns compressing sharply above $750M — the scale that looks like success is often the thing destroying it. Paul Tudor Jones grounds all of this in the individual: none of it matters without risk management first. Together they argue that markets reward clarity and discipline, not storytelling or scale for its own sake.
