# Compute Scarcity, Safety as Strategy, Model Commoditization Debate

These sources converge on three fault lines shaping the AI industry. On compute: OpenAI's CFO flatly states demand will outstrip supply through 2026, while Anthropic's CFO describes compute procurement as existential — buy too much and go bankrupt, too little and fall behind the frontier. On safety-as-positioning: one source argues Anthropic's safety branding is a calculated move to delegitimize competitors and consolidate control, a claim made sharper by the observation that their safety framework structurally outputs self-serving conclusions. On commoditization: Anthropic's own revenue tripling in four months — from $9B to $30B run rate — directly rebuts the narrative that frontier models are undifferentiated utilities, though Bill Maris raises a genuine counterpoint: Google could slash token prices 80% and destabilize both OpenAI and Anthropic overnight. The deeper tension is whether frontier intelligence is a durable moat or a temporary lead before infrastructure economics crush margins.
