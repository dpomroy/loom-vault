# Problem Selection Over Problem Solving

These quotes converge on a counterintuitive claim: the bottleneck in exceptional work is choosing what to work on, not execution. Most people — even smart ones — are conservative about problem selection while being inventive in solving whatever problem lands in front of them. This mismatch is costly. A genuinely new idea must look bad to most observers, or it would already be occupied territory — meaning the selection of problems requires tolerating apparent foolishness. Curiosity is offered as the corrective: not a vague feeling, but a precise instrument that navigates toward underexplored gaps. Planning, by contrast, only works for goals you can already describe, which excludes most discoveries worth making.
