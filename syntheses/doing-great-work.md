# Curiosity, Morale, and the Problem-Selection Edge

Both sources converge on intrinsic drive as the foundation of exceptional work, but they approach it differently. The 'How to Do Great Work' essay frames curiosity as simultaneously motivational fuel and navigational compass — it doesn't just push you forward, it steers you toward the right problems. Crucially, the essay argues that problem selection matters more than problem solving, a counterintuitive claim most high performers neglect. The morale-compounds-via-work insight adds a systems dynamic: output quality and mental state are in a feedback loop, not a one-way relationship. Kelly Johnson's career illustrates all of this lived out: decades of turning down prestigious promotions to keep doing the actual work he chose at age twelve. Where the essay theorizes about curiosity as a guide, Johnson embodies it as a decades-long commitment.
