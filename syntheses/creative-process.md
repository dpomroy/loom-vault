# Vision Uncompromised: When Creators Refuse the Middle

These sources converge on a shared belief: great creative work requires protecting a singular vision against compromise, committee, and data-driven caution. Disney's 'no compromise on money, talent, or time' for Snow White and Kelly Johnson's contempt for design-by-committee both argue that brilliance demands concentrated authority and radical commitment. Zelnick sharpens the commercial stakes — data is backward-looking, so truly unexpected hits can never be fully engineered. Rubin adds a subtler dimension: the creative act isn't just bold initiation but active protection. The moment of discovery is fragile, and the rest of the process is defending it from dilution. Where Disney and Johnson emphasize scale and control, Rubin locates the real threat as internal — the drift toward refinement that quietly kills what made something alive.
