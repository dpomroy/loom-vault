# Control, IP, and the Architecture of Power

These sources collectively reveal that media and sports empires are built not on assets or talent, but on structural control. Ecclestone never owned Formula 1 yet never lost control of it — power came from information asymmetry and relationship leverage. The NFL's Rozelle similarly oriented everything around league-level stature rather than individual teams, creating a collective machine. The record industry's grip worked the same way: keep artists ignorant of their own fan relationships and they remain dependent. Disney's parks outcome shows what happens when IP control compounds over decades — $10 billion in annual profit from what began as a creative obsession. The throughline is deliberate architecture: who controls the relationship with the audience ultimately captures the economics.
