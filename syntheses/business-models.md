# Structure Determines Strategy, Not the Reverse

These sources collectively argue that a company's ownership and financial architecture isn't incidental — it dictates everything downstream. Vanguard's mutual structure made low-cost indexing not just possible but inevitable: with no external shareholders to satisfy, cost reduction became the only logical strategy. Costco's membership model similarly transforms the P&L — when 70% of operating income comes from fees, the retail floor can run near breakeven, making price-gouging structurally irrational rather than just culturally resisted. Rolex's foundation structure removes quarterly pressure entirely, enabling decade-long brand patience. Hyperliquid inverts the FTX lesson: by ensuring their liquidity pool isn't load-bearing to operations, they avoid the conflict of interest that destroyed a competitor. Across all four, structure isn't a legal formality — it's the actual moat.
