# Structural Alignment: How Ownership Shape Determines Strategy

A recurring insight across these sources is that organizational structure isn't neutral — it actively determines what strategies are even possible. Vanguard's mutualized structure made low-cost indexing not just an option but an inevitability: 'strategy follows structure.' Rolex's foundation ownership eliminates quarterly pressure entirely, letting management play a genuinely longer game than public competitors can. Costco embodies 'scale economies shared with customers' — a flywheel only sustainable because management resists the heroin of easy margin expansion. Google represents the sharpest case: its structural advantages compound in both directions simultaneously, cutting costs while lifting revenue per search. The common thread isn't any single model, but that durable businesses encode their values into structure before competition forces compromise.
