# Small Funds, Short Traps, and the IPO Dilemma

These sources converge on a shared skepticism toward conventional market wisdom, but from very different angles. Bill Maris makes the mathematical case that large venture funds are structurally incapable of returning meaningful multiples — the exit market simply cannot absorb the capital. Dan Loeb warns that 'safe' shorts on dominant companies like Nvidia or Amazon have historically destroyed short-sellers, suggesting market consensus around obvious trades is a trap. The secondaries panel argues there's no longer a good reason to stay private longer, pushing back on the trend of late-stage private financing. Paul Tudor Jones anchors all of this: superior risk management, not superior insight, is the actual differentiator across every strategy.
