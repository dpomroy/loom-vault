# Compute, Scaling, and the Race to Frontier

These sources converge on one striking reality: compute is the binding constraint and primary competitive weapon in AI right now. Anthropic's CFO frames the compute decision as existential — too much and you go bankrupt, too little and you fall behind the frontier. OpenAI's CFO confirms this from the other side, noting that even $120B+ in raised capital won't yield enough compute through 2026. The growth numbers underneath this constraint are almost incomprehensible: Anthropic went from $9B to $30B ARR in a single quarter, prompting one investor to note nothing like it has ever occurred in capitalism. What these sources don't fully resolve is whether this spending race is sustainable discipline or the early architecture of a bubble.
