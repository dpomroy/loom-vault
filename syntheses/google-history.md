# Google Won by Letting Users Leave Fastest

The through-line across these sources is that Google's dominance came from a philosophical inversion: where portals wanted users to stay, Google optimized for users to leave satisfied. The portal companies failed not from lack of resources but from a business model that made good search self-defeating — keeping eyeballs meant tolerating mediocre results. Google's ad ranking innovation reinforced this: click-through rate as a relevance proxy meant commercial and user interests aligned, rather than conflicted. The deeper pattern, captured in the PC-to-web-to-mobile arc, is that Google understood each platform transition as a distribution problem before competitors recognized there was a transition happening at all.
