# Compute Scarcity, Revenue Explosions, and Who Captures Value

Anthropic's CFO frames compute as existential — buy too much and you go bankrupt, too little and you fall behind the frontier. That knife-edge constraint is validated by the revenue numbers: $9B to $30B run rate in four months, a scale Gavin Baker calls unprecedented in the history of capitalism. Yet Nikesh Arora cuts against the infrastructure euphoria with a structural claim: models will commoditize into a utility layer, and the real profit pools sit in applications built on top. This creates a tension none of these sources fully resolve — Anthropic's current dominance is real and accelerating, but the question of whether frontier model builders or application-layer companies ultimately capture the surplus remains genuinely open.
