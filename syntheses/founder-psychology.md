# Hunger, Identity, and the Founder's Inner Engine

Schwarzenegger locates his drive explicitly in emotional deprivation — unmet childhood needs converted into an obsessive hunger for recognition and achievement. This is the rawest articulation of a pattern that appears more subtly elsewhere. The Jobs sources reveal a different inversion: his identity was never 'businessman,' yet he could do nothing but lead — drive untethered from conventional ambition. Kareem Amin identifies the same wound operating at the company level, recognizing a scarcity mindset shaping his business decisions and consciously deciding to break from it. Together these sources argue that founder psychology is less about confidence or optimism than about a specific relationship to lack — and whether that relationship remains unconscious or gets examined.
