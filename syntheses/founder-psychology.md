# Hunger as Identity: What Drives Obsessive Builders

These sources converge on a striking idea: the drive to build isn't a choice but a condition — rooted in psychology, sometimes in deprivation. Schwarzenegger traces his hunger directly to emotional absence in childhood; the lack of love became fuel for achievement. Phil Knight describes total life imbalance at Nike's founding not as sacrifice but as preference — he wanted more of it. Jonathan Ross frames the same drive as structural discontent, a personality feature required to keep pushing. Knight then reframes the whole thing as vocation rather than career. Where they differ: Schwarzenegger locates the source in wound and compensation, Ross in temperament, Knight in meaning-seeking. Together they suggest obsessive building is less about opportunity than about who someone already is before the company exists.
