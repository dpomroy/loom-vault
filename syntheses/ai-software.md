# Google Invented the Future, Then Flinched

These sources collectively reveal a painful irony at the heart of the AI revolution: the company that built the foundational technology — the Transformer — spent five years failing to capitalize on it, while a startup it helped train (OpenAI) launched a product that redefined consumer expectations overnight. What makes this more than a simple cautionary tale is the mechanism: Google's delay wasn't ignorance but structural conflict. Protecting $140B in Search revenue made aggressive AI deployment feel dangerous rather than urgent. Anthropic's numbers — $9B to $30B run rate in four months — show what happens when that constraint doesn't exist. The innovator's dilemma, usually an abstraction, here has a dollar figure attached.
