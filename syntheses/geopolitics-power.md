# Adversaries Think in Decades; Democracies Think in Cycles

A consistent thread across these sources is that authoritarian and adversarial states operate with longer time horizons and fewer internal contradictions than democracies. China's alleged 20-year plan to reshape Western institutions exploited exactly the short-termism that democratic politics produces. Farber's framing of dictators as simultaneously strong and weak adds crucial nuance: that structural illegitimacy breeds paranoia and corruption, making authoritarian militaries brittle under real pressure. The AI-as-national-security argument sharpens this into a binary — you either build the capability or you don't survive. Fetterman's point about data center moratoriums playing into China's hands brings it to domestic policy: America's internal political dysfunction is itself a geopolitical gift to adversaries.
