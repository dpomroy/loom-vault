# Originality Lives in Problem Selection, Not Solutions

There's strong convergence across sources that originality is less about technique than about where you point your attention. The argument that problem selection matters more than problem solving reframes creative courage as an upstream choice — most people apply creativity only after the question is already settled. The observation that good ideas must seem bad to most people adds a corollary: consensus-seeking kills originality before it starts. Rubin's outsider ignorance producing authentic hip-hop and Naval's 'escape competition through authenticity' both arrive at the same place from different angles — genuine originality isn't strategic differentiation, it's the natural output of a perspective nobody else occupies.
