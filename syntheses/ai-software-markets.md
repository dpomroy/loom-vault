# Coding as AI's Commercial Unlock, Models as Utilities

Multiple sources converge on coding as the decisive commercial breakthrough for AI — not chatbots or general assistants, but code generation that flipped from 20% to near-total AI authorship almost overnight. Anthropic's CFO confirms this materially: model intelligence leaps unlock TAM in ways that weren't predicted, with revenue running from $9B to $30B in a single quarter largely on the back of developer adoption. Yet there's meaningful tension on where the money ultimately lands. One view holds that models will commoditize into a utility layer, with profit pools migrating to applications built on top. Another view, backed by Anthropic's actual trajectory, suggests frontier model providers aren't commoditizing — they're compounding. The compute constraint (OpenAI won't have enough even in 2026) further complicates the utility narrative: you can't treat something as infrastructure when you can't yet reliably provision it.
