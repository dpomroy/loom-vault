# Small Teams, High Talent, No Committees

These sources converge on a single uncomfortable truth: organizational excellence requires deliberate restraint — fewer people, higher standards, clearer principles. Kelly Johnson's indictment of design-by-committee captures why consensus produces mediocrity rather than brilliance, while his rule on restricting headcount 'in an almost vicious manner' is the structural mechanism that prevents it. Koch's principle-based management targets the same problem from a cultural angle — building organizations where people know what to do without being told, eliminating the need for committees in the first place. Foroughi adds the talent-density corollary: A-players don't just outperform, they're actively degraded by surrounding mediocrity. Together they argue organizational drag isn't accidental — it's what happens when leaders optimize for comfort over capability.
