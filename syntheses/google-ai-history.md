# Google Built AI's Foundation, Then Froze

These sources collectively tell a story of a company that repeatedly invented the future — PageRank, distributed computing, the Transformer — only to struggle capturing it. Google invented the dominant architecture underlying every modern AI system, then spent five years not fully seizing it. The pattern echoes its own history: earlier sources show how portals couldn't pivot to search because of ad revenue lock-in; now Google faces the same trap in reverse, where its $140B search business makes aggressive AI disruption existential. What distinguishes Google from IBM or Microsoft — both of whom lost dominance in subsequent platform shifts — is that Google actually held its position through mobile. Whether AI breaks that streak is the live question.
