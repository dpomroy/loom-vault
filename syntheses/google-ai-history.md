# Google Invented AI's Foundation, Then Froze

These sources collectively trace a sharp irony: Google built the foundational technology of the modern AI era — the Transformer — then spent five years failing to capitalize on it. Publishing 'Attention Is All You Need' was simultaneously a gift to humanity and a strategic blunder, seeding every competitor. The historical pattern makes this more striking: unlike IBM and Microsoft, Google survived its first platform transition (web to mobile), yet the AI shift proved harder precisely because it threatens the search ad engine that funds everything. What distinguishes Google's current position, though, is structural — it's the only player with a profitable core business, proprietary AI chips, and frontier models, making its dilemma both more acute and more survivable than any predecessor faced.
