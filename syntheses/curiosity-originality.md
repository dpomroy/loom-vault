# Problem Choice Beats Problem Solving

These quotes converge on a counterintuitive claim: the bottleneck in great work isn't execution but direction. Curiosity isn't just motivating fuel — it actively navigates, steering you toward what's worth working on in the first place. The sharpest insight is that people apply originality generously to solutions while staying oddly conservative about which problems to attempt at all. Meanwhile, the critique of planning cuts deeper: goals you can name in advance are precisely the ones that don't require discovery. Originality as a 'habit of mind' rather than a process ties this together — it suggests the capacity can't be switched on strategically, only cultivated through genuine engagement.
