# AI's Infrastructure Bottleneck: Compute as the Binding Constraint

Multiple sources converge on a striking claim: compute is not just a cost — it's an existential constraint that determines who wins in AI. Anthropic's CFO frames it as a knife's edge: too much compute and you go bankrupt, too little and you fall off the frontier. OpenAI's CFO says even by 2026, supply still won't be sufficient. Google's token processing jumped 50x in a single year, illustrating demand that outruns any reasonable planning horizon. Alex Sacerdote adds the hardware dimension: AI workloads are pushing every physical limit of existing silicon. Where sources diverge is on what this means strategically — Anthropic treats compute scarcity as a capital allocation puzzle, while Sacerdote and the Etched founders see it as the central structural investment thesis of the decade.
