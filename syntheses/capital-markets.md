# Private Markets Are Rewriting the Rules of Capital

There's a structural tension running through these sources about how capital actually flows versus how it's supposed to. Bill Maris demonstrates mathematically that large venture funds are structurally incapable of generating top returns — the exit market simply can't absorb what a $7B fund needs to return. Jeremy Giffon adds a darker wrinkle: fund selection is driven by storytelling, not track records, because realized returns take a decade to materialize. Meanwhile, the secondary market source shows that staying private longer has created a parallel liquidity system where secondaries now rival IPOs as exit mechanisms. Thomas Laffont's 'public market as great antiseptic' cuts against all of this — suggesting that price discovery and accountability only truly arrive when companies face public scrutiny. Each source diagnoses a different dysfunction in the same broken capital allocation machine.
