# Cost, Structure, and the Index Fund Heresy

These quotes trace the internal logic of Vanguard's founding and its market vindication. The structural argument came first — Bogle's challenge to the industry's accepted ownership model was a precondition for everything else. The cost argument followed directly from that structure: because fees compound against investors over time, minimizing them isn't a nicety but the central mechanism of wealth creation. Buffett's tribute confirms the real-world scale of that insight. The most pointed tension is in quote four — the industry's instinct that 'average returns' would never satisfy investors turned out to be wrong, because after costs, most active managers delivered worse than average. The skeptics provided the resistance that proved the thesis.
